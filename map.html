Interactive maps of Civil War - How the South Went Down with a Fight
Other Section 1, Max Froch + Aiden Tepper

The goal of the South during the Civil War was to maintain its independence and defend its boundaries from the Union. While this was not the outcome, the South was no easy opponent. The two maps above demonstrate the strength of our state and how it maintained itself under all types of attacks.

The first map is a representation of the first major battle of the Civil War: the First Battle of Bull Run. Brigadier General McDowell's poorly executed surprise attack on the Confederates, in an attempt to seize Richmond and end the war, became a bloody battle that ended with Confederate victory.

The second map represents the Unionâ€™s Peninsula Campaign: a major operation with the end objective of capturing Richmond and ending the war. Under the orders of Major General McClellan, this too was supposed to end in victory for the Union, but yet again, Virginia held strong, this time under the orders of General Robert E. Lee after Johnston was injured. Because of Lee's more aggressive approach, the Union retreated and left the Confederates to another victory. The South was outnumbered and the war ended in loss for the South, but their efforts went above and beyond what was expected by the Union.
